Research

A research program for undercounted value and human-centered coordination.

This framing makes Echoin legible as a serious research direction rather than a purely visionary statement.

Research question

Can a protocol be designed that records and circulates translation, mediation, trust formation, reuse, and interpretive labor in ways that are legible to people, useful to communities, and conditionally connectable to economic life without collapsing into pure speculation?

Initial hypotheses

H1

People perform socially useful work that markets systematically undercount.

H2

These forms of value can be recorded more clearly with structured contribution categories and contextual records.

H3

Internal circulation and access rights can be tested before open monetization.

Minimum prototype

  • Contribution categories: translation, mediation, review, reuse support, care, curation, interpretive synthesis
  • Contextual records: what happened, for whom, under what conditions
  • Trust and usefulness annotations
  • Internal exchange for access, time, priority, visibility, or collaboration rights
  • Optional external sale of resulting services or rights

Metrics

Legibility

Whether users feel their contributions are more accurately seen.

Circulation

Whether internal exchange leads to reuse, collaboration, or support.

Economic bridge

Whether recorded value can connect to paid work without immediately becoming speculative.

This starter is designed as a calm front door: concept first, technical layers second, detailed materials on request.
